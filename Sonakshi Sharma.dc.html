02 The Premise

Three questions I keep returning to.

i.

How do we build a system an expert is willing to trust — and right to?

On calibration, explanation, and the long distance between a model that is confident and a model that is correct.

ii.

Where should the human stay in the loop, and where should they step out?

Designing the seam between human judgment and machine inference so each does only what it is genuinely best at.

iii.

Can a model learn the shape of the future from the texture of the past?

On forecasting, synthetic data, and what it takes to teach a system to reason honestly about time.

A Position
What I believe

Intelligence worth deploying doesn't replace the expert in the room. It makes their judgment sharper, faster, and more accountable.

Every system I build starts from that conviction — legible by design, calibrated about its own uncertainty, and answerable to the person who carries the consequences.

08 Beyond

Beyond the notebook

I collect first sentences.

The best papers and the best novels open the same way — they make a promise. I keep a file of openings that earned my attention.

I think best on bad trains.

Some problems only loosen when I stop staring at them. Most of my better ideas arrived between stations, on the back of a receipt.

I think research should have a point of view.

Neutrality is rarely honest. The work I admire most takes a position about how the world ought to work, then earns it.
