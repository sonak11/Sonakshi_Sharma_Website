07

Beyond the resume

I collect first sentences.

The best papers and the best novels open the same way — they make a promise. I keep a file of openings that earned my attention.

I think best on bad trains.

Some problems only loosen when I stop staring at them. Most of my better ideas arrived between stations, on the back of a receipt.

I think research should have a point of view.

Neutrality is rarely honest. The work I admire most takes a position about how the world ought to work, then earns it.
